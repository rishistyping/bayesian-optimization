Start with what you believed before testimony

A friend tells you it's raining outside. You haven't looked outside. You only have their word.

How much should you believe them? Don't ask yes or no; ask how confident you should be.

Start with your starting belief (base rate) before testimony. This can come from season, location, or recent weather.

Testimony as a channel

You see things. You remember things. People tell you things. These all act like channels that pass information into your judgment.

Testimony is a channel too. It depends on how often a person is right when it rains, and how often they cry rain when it doesn't. Those two numbers determine how informative testimony is.

Forced update

Once you set a prior and channel reliability, the posterior is not a guess. Consistency requires it. Once inputs are fixed, the answer is forced.

In the canonical setup, the posterior lands at 78.5% after testimony. Using symbols: P(R)=0.30, P(T|R)=0.85, P(T|\neg R)=0.10, so P(R|T)=0.785.

Why We Fail

Try presets and predict first: will testimony move belief a lot or a little? Reliable testimony shifts belief a lot; unreliable testimony moves it only a little.

You don't jump to 85%. You don't stay at 30%. You land where both prior and evidence agree.

Very reliable friend target: 95.3% (0.953). Unreliable friend target: 39.1% (0.391).
