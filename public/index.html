Start with what you believed before testimony

A friend tells you it's raining outside. You haven't looked outside. You only have their word.

How much should you believe them? Don't ask yes or no; ask how confident you should be. Bayesian updating treats confidence as a probability estimate, not a binary verdict.

[1]. Your prior is the base rate; Bayes updating starts from that baseline, not from zero.

Source: Bayes' theorem (SEP); Bayes theorem in diagnosis (NCBI).

Start with your starting belief (base rate) before testimony. This can come from season, location, or recent weather.[1]

Testimony as a channel

You see things. You remember things. People tell you things. These all act like channels that pass information into your judgment.

[2]. P(T|R) and P(T|\neg R) work like sensitivity and false-alarm behavior in diagnostic reasoning.

Source: Bayes theorem in diagnosis (NCBI); Likelihood ratios and testing (NCBI).

Testimony is a channel too. It depends on how often a person is right when it rains, and how often they cry rain when it doesn't. Those two numbers determine how informative testimony is.[2] P(T|R) is not the same as P(R|T); Bayes' rule is the bridge between them.

Forced update

Once you set a prior and channel reliability, the posterior is not a guess. Consistency requires it. Once inputs are fixed, the answer is forced.

[3]. Once prior and likelihood terms are fixed, the posterior is mathematically determined.

Source: Bayes' theorem (SEP); Bayes theorem in diagnosis (NCBI).

In the canonical setup, the posterior lands at 78.5% after testimony. Using symbols: P(R)=0.30, P(T|R)=0.85, P(T|\neg R)=0.10, so P(R|T)=0.785.[3]

Conditional perspective

[4]. Conditioning renormalizes probability inside the subset where the condition is true.

Source: Bayes' theorem (SEP); Bayes theorem in diagnosis (NCBI).

Conditional probability means we shrink attention to a subset of days. In Given testimony, the denominator is only days where testimony says rain.[4] Conditioning renormalizes probabilities inside the chosen subset so the total within that subset sums to 1.

Posterior is the rainy share inside that testimony subset. Use the three sliders above; this view updates live.

Why We Fail

[5]. People often underweight base rates; natural-frequency representations improve Bayesian judgments.

Source: Tversky & Kahneman reference index (PubMed); Gigerenzer & Hoffrage (DOI).

Try presets and predict first: will testimony move belief a lot or a little? Reliable testimony shifts belief a lot; unreliable testimony moves it only a little.[5]

You don't jump to 85%. You don't stay at 30%. You land where both prior and evidence agree.

Very reliable friend target: 95.3% (0.953). Unreliable friend target: 39.1% (0.391).
